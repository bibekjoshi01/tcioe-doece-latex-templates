\subsection{Motivation}

Indoor air quality has a direct impact on human health, productivity, and overall 
well-being, yet it is often overlooked compared to outdoor air pollution. 
Many indoor spaces, such as classrooms, offices, and laboratories, suffer 
from inadequate ventilation and accumulation of pollutants like CO₂, 
particulate matter, and volatile organic compounds. Existing commercial 
monitoring solutions are either expensive or lack integration with automated 
control systems, limiting their accessibility and effectiveness. This project 
aims to address these gaps by developing a cost-effective, real-time indoor 
air quality monitoring and control system. By combining sensor technology, 
IoT connectivity, and automated control, the system will enable timely alerts 
and corrective actions, fostering healthier indoor environments while providing 
students with hands-on experience in electronics, data acquisition, and automation.
